What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value of a person's weight in relation to their height. It is a simple tool that helps to categorize individuals into different weight categories, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese.

The formula to calculate BMI is: BMI = (weight in kilograms) / (height in meters)^2

BMI provides a rough estimate of body fat and is often used as a screening tool for potential health risks associated with being underweight or overweight.

BMI Scale

Underweight: less than 18.5

Normal Weight: 18.5 - 24.9

Overweight: 25.0 - 29.9

Obese: 30.0 or greater
